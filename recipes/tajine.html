The Moroccan Tajine is a slow-cooked, flavorful stew made with tender meat (often chicken or lamb), a rich blend of spices like cumin, turmeric, and cinnamon, and a mix of vegetables or dried fruits. Cooked in a traditional clay pot, the tajine is a symbol of Moroccan hospitality and comfort food at its finest. ğŸ‹ğŸ—ğŸ¥•âœ¨

  1. Prepare the meat: Cut the chicken (or lamb/beef) into pieces and season with salt, pepper, cumin, turmeric, ginger, cinnamon, and garlic.
  2. Heat the oil: In a tajine or a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Cook the onions: Add chopped onions and cook until soft and translucent.
  4. Brown the meat: Add the meat pieces to the pot and cook until browned on all sides.
  5. Add the olives and preserved lemons: Stir in olives, preserved lemon slices, and a handful of cilantro/parsley.
  6. Add water: Pour in enough water to just cover the meat. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  7. Simmer: Cover and let the dish simmer for about 1-1.5 hours or until the meat is tender and cooked through.
  8. Add vegetables (optional): If you're using potatoes, carrots, or other vegetables, add them about 30 minutes before the end of cooking.
  9. Add dried fruits (optional): If using dried fruits like apricots or prunes, add them in the last 15 minutes of cooking.
  10. Garnish and serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and parsley, and serve hot with couscous or bread.
