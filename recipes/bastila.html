Bastila

Bastila is a delicious Moroccan pastry, often filled with spiced chicken or pigeon, almonds, and eggs, wrapped in thin layers of dough and then baked until golden. It’s a perfect blend of savory and sweet flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (or pigeon, if you prefer the traditional version)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp saffron threads (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup almonds, toasted and chopped
  • 6 eggs, beaten
  • 1 pack of phyllo dough (or warqa, traditional Moroccan dough)
  • Powdered sugar (for garnish)
  • Cinnamon (for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a large pot, cook the chicken with onion, garlic, ginger, cinnamon, saffron (if using), salt, pepper, and enough water to cover the chicken. Simmer for 45 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked.
  2. Remove the chicken, shred the meat, and set it aside. Reserve the cooking liquid and reduce it to a thick sauce.
  3. In a pan, sauté the shredded chicken with cilantro and a bit of olive oil. Add the reduced cooking sauce to the chicken mixture. Let it cook for a few minutes until the flavors meld together.
  4. In a separate pan, scramble the beaten eggs and cook them until just set, then mix them into the chicken mixture along with the chopped almonds.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place a layer of phyllo dough in a pie pan, brushing each sheet with olive oil. Spoon the chicken mixture into the center.
  6. Fold the dough over the filling, layering more sheets of phyllo dough, brushing each one with olive oil, and folding over the edges to seal the filling.
  7. Bake for 30–40 minutes, until the phyllo dough is golden brown and crispy.
  8. Once out of the oven, sprinkle powdered sugar and cinnamon on top. Serve warm and enjoy!
